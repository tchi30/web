Important Rules


  • The player must dribble the ball with one hand while moving both feet. A double dribble is a violation where a player dribbles a basketball, picks it up with both hands, then dribbles again.
  • The ball must stay in bounds. If it goes out of bounds, it's given to the opposite team of the team who's player last touched the ball.
  • The player's hand must be on top of the ball while dribbling, or else a carry violation is called, and the ball is given to the other team.
  • A travel violation is called when a player takes more than two steps without dribbling the ball.
  • Fouls:
    1. Holding foul: When a player grabs their opponent in an attempt to prevent them from moving forward
    2. Hand checking: When a player continually keeps their hands on an opposing player.
    3. Reaching foul: When a defender attempts to reach in and steal the ball from the offensive player, but makes contact with the player's arms and impedes the ball handler's progress.
    4. Blocking foul: When a defender makes contact with an offensive player without giving proper space.
    5. Technical foul: When a player or coach displays unsportsmanship-like behavior, such as loud swearing, arguing with the referee, fighting with a fellow player, etc.
    6. Flagrant foul: When a player performs a serious act of violence that injures or harms other players.
