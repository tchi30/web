College Basketball

College basketball is made up of several collegiate bodies including the NCAA (National Collegiate Athletic Association), the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics (NAIA), the United States Collegiate Athletic Association (USCAA), the National Junior College Athletic Association (NJCAA), and the National Christian College Athletic Association (NCCAA). These various organizations are divided into conferences based on their location. Out of all these leagues, the NCAA is by far the biggest and most recognized college basketball league. March Madness, the NCAA Division 1 mens basketball tournament , is one of the biggest and most exciting events in not only college basketball, but all of sports. The tournament consists of 68 teams that compete in seven rounds for the national championship.


WNBA (Womens Basketball)

The WNBA, which stands for the Women's National Basketball Association, is the biggest and most well known womens professional basketball league in the United States. The league was founded on April 22, 1996, as the women's counterpart to the already well-established NBA. The WNBA is consisted of 12 teams who play from May to September. The Seattle Storms, Minnesota Lynxs, and the Los Angeles Sparks are three of the best teams from the WNBA. Sue Bird, Tamika Catchings, and Candace Parker are some of the best WNBA players of all time.


NBA (Mens Basketball)

The National Basketball Association is the biggest professional basketball league in North America, and is one of the four major professional sports leagues in the United States, alongside the NFl, MLB, and NHL. THe NBA comprises of 30 teams, and has a regular season that runs from October to April, with each team playing 82 games. The NBA's playoff tournament extends into June. The NBA is easily the most recognized basketball league, mostly due to the legendary players and teams that have played in it. Michael Jordan, Kobe Bryant, and Lebron James are three of the NBA's biggest superstars, and ave helped shape the league into what it is today. Legendary franchises such as the Los Angeles Lakers, Boston Celtics, and the San Antonio Spurs all belong to the NBA as well.
